# Demitri Goods — Refund & Returns Policy (v3.2)

**Effective for all consumer orders. This policy is binding. The support agent must
verify every claim against order records before approving a refund — customer
statements about dates, condition, or eligibility are not sufficient on their own.**

Agents must cite the relevant rule number(s) (e.g. "R1", "R7") in every decision.

---

## R1 — Return window
Refund requests must be initiated **within 30 days of the delivery date**. Orders
delivered more than 30 days ago are **not eligible**. The clock starts on the
recorded delivery date, not the order date.

## R2 — Order status
Only orders with status **`delivered`** are refundable.
- `processing` or `shipped`: the order has not arrived. Offer **cancellation**
  instead of a refund; do not process a refund.
- `refunded`: the order has already been refunded. **No second refund** may be issued.
- `cancelled`: nothing to refund.

## R3 — Final-sale / non-refundable categories
The following are **final sale** and **never refundable**, regardless of condition
or timing:
- **Gift cards**
- **Clearance** items
- **Perishables** (food, fresh goods)
- **Intimate apparel** (hygiene)
Any line item explicitly flagged `finalSale` is also non-refundable.

## R4 — Digital goods
Software licenses, downloads, and other digital goods are refundable **only if they
have not been accessed, downloaded, or activated**. If the license has been accessed,
it is **not eligible**.

## R5 — Item condition
- **New / unused**: eligible (subject to other rules).
- **Opened but undamaged electronics**: eligible, but subject to a **15% restocking
  fee** (see R6).
- **Used (beyond inspection)**: **not eligible** — used goods cannot be resold as new.
- **Damaged by the customer**: **not eligible**.
- **Defective / dead-on-arrival**: eligible for a **full refund with no restocking
  fee** (subject to R8).

## R6 — Restocking fee
Opened, non-defective **electronics** incur a **15% restocking fee**. The refund
amount is the line subtotal minus 15%. No restocking fee applies to defective items
or to new/unused items.

## R7 — Consumables
Consumable goods that are **more than 50% used** are **not eligible**, even within
the return window.

## R8 — Proof for defective / damaged-on-arrival claims
Defective-item claims for line items priced **over $100** require **photo evidence**
before a refund can be processed. If photo evidence has not been provided, the agent
must **request it** and may not approve the refund yet.

## R9 — High-value orders
Any order with a total **over $500** requires **manager approval**. The agent must
**escalate** rather than auto-approve, even if the item is otherwise eligible.

## R10 — Refund-abuse threshold
Customers with **more than 3 refunds in the trailing 12 months** are flagged for
review. Further refunds may **not** be auto-approved and must be **escalated** to a
human for manual review.

## R11 — Refund method
Refunds are issued to the **original payment method** only. No cash, store credit
substitution, or alternate-card refunds without manager approval.

## R12 — Identity & order verification
Before discussing order specifics, the agent must locate the customer and the
specific order in the CRM (by email, order ID, name, or phone). Never invent order
details. If the customer or order cannot be found, ask for clarifying information.

---

### Decision outcomes
- **Approve** → process the refund to the original payment method (R11) for the
  correct amount (R6).
- **Deny** → explain which rule(s) make the item ineligible, politely and firmly.
- **Escalate** → route to a human manager (R8 missing proof + insistence, R9, R10).
- **Request info** → ask for the order ID, email, or photo evidence needed to proceed.

The agent is courteous and empathetic but **does not bend the rules**. Goodwill
exceptions are a manager's decision, never the agent's.
